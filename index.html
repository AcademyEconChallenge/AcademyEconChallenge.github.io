High-school students have access to an education in economics through AP programs. However, they do not have the ability to apply learned concepts to the real world, which is a fundamental aspect of economics. In an effort to enhance the high-school economics experience for a variety of students, Bergen County Academies will be hosting its first ever Economics Challenge.

High-schools in Bergen County will have the opportunity to bring teams consisting of three students who would participate in a two part competition. Firstly, BCA Economics Challenge contains a comprehensive, multiple-choice exam containing both microeconomic and macroeconomic components. The second part of the competition, the highlight of the event, is a case-analysis of a real-life situation involving various economic components. To detail, a question can involve the best method of addressing the need for affordable apartments in New York City; various options can include setting a price floor, subsidizing construction workers to build new homes, or offering tax breaks to accommodative building owners. Of course, the repercussions of these actions must be examined from all perspectives, and it is the ability of a team to understand the consequences of an action that will be focused on during grading. Only being a one day event, BCA Economics Challenge is a great way for students interested in economics to flex their skills and participate in a new and evolving event.
