The Challenge

High-school students have access to an education in economics through AP programs. However, they do not have the ability to apply learned concepts to the real world, which is a fundamental aspect of economics. In an effort to enhance the high-school economics experience for a variety of students, Bergen County Academies will be hosting its first ever Economics Challenge.
Only being a one day event, the Academy Economics Challenge is a great way for students interested in economics to flex their skills and participate in a new and evolving event.
We ask students interested in general economics, macroeconomic or microeconomic analysis, and public policy to step forward. You will be competing in teams against similarly inclined participants from other schools. Give the dismal science a shot.

Competition Format

Competitors are invited from high schools all around Bergen County.
Students will form teams of 3 to compete in the two parts of the Challenge.

  1. A comprehensive, multiple-choice exam on microeconomics, macroeconomics, international and developmental economics, along with current events.
  2. The second part of the competition, the highlight of the event, is a case-analysis of a real-life situation involving various economic components.
    For example, a question can involve the best method of addressing the need for affordable apartments in New York City; various options can include setting a price floor, subsidizing construction workers to build new homes, or offering tax breaks for accommodating building owners.
